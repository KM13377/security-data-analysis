# European Defence Spending Analysis

## Objective

Analyze defence expenditure trends among selected European NATO members between 2014 and 2025.

## Research Question

How did NATO's eastern flank countries adjust defence spending after the deterioration of the European security environment?

## Data Source

NATO Defence Expenditure Report (2014–2025)

## Methodology

Countries analyzed:
- Poland
- Germany
- France

Indicator:
- Defence expenditure as percentage of GDP

Comparison:
- 2014 vs 2025

## Results

| Country | 2014 | 2025 |
|----------|----------|----------|
| Poland | 1.86 | 4.48 |
| Germany | 1.16 | 2.00 (2024) |
| France | 1.81 | 2.05 |

## Key Findings

- Poland recorded the largest increase in defence spending.
- Germany reached the NATO 2% benchmark after years of lower expenditure.
- France maintained consistently high military spending.
- NATO's eastern flank demonstrated the strongest growth in defence effort.

## Strategic Assessment

The Russian invasion of Ukraine accelerated defence modernization and military investment across Europe, particularly among states geographically exposed to potential threats.
